Governor’s Project

Our District Governor, Gabriella Slootsky, has announced her District Wide Project! I am proud to present, Soles4Souls. Souls4Soles is a nonprofit organization that focuses on collecting unwanted or discarded clothes and providing them to people in need. With over 70 million pairs of shoes doanted, Soles4Souls has provided millions with new clothes and prevented the production of waste that would have resulted with the shoes being discarded. I genuinely, wholeheartly recommend and encourage you to donate at least one of your older pair of shoes that don’t fit you anymore. Save waste, make smiles.

District Project

I also am proud to announce this service year’s District Project, Pencils of Promise! Pencils of Promise works internationally to build schools for communities that need it. Pencils for Promise has a profound impact on people all around the worl, due to the very nature of its goal.. The opportunity for education is a amenity that people in developed countries take for granted. Education can provide the chance for families to create new beginnings and creep out of poverty. As they say, knowledge is power, and in a growing service based economy, it is ever more important an essential to get an education to survive.

School House

Key Club has partnered up with Schoolhouse to provide Key Clubbers with an exciting volunteering opportunity. Schoolhouse is a free, virtual math peer-tutoring platform created by Sal Khan of Khan Academy, where you have the opportunity to become a tutor. Instructions to sign up are as follows.

1. Visit this link ( bit.ly/3KEJsId ) and create an account. Remember to select your club and district.

2. Click the “Become a Tutor” tab and follow the checklist — you’ll certify your mastery in the math courses you’ve taken, complete our tutor training, and get matched with an onboarding buddy who will help you host your first session.

3. Once you’ve become a Schoolhouse tutor, you can host sessions based on whatever best fits your schedule. Schoolhouse is always looking for more tutors to host Live Help, where students drop in 7-10 p.m. EDT to ask any math questions they have. If you’re not a planner, you can even jump in at the last minute to host these sessions on any days you’re free. Click this link ( https://bit.ly/384ZMEK ) if you are interested.

4. Encourage your friends to become tutors too. You can see how your club and district are doing on the Key Club Leaderboard at this link ( https://bit.ly/3y5RreM ). Schoolhouse will recognize the districts and clubs that have the highest impact (see more information below).
