LTC Information

We are excited to announce that registration for the Leadership Training Conference (LTC) in 2023 is now open! The conference will be held from March 31st to April 2nd at the Desmond Hotel in Albany, and it promises to be a weekend full of fun, service, and learning. At the LTC, you'll have the opportunity to gain skills that you can apply personally and professionally through workshops, hear from an inspiring keynote speaker, perform service alongside Key Club members from across the state, celebrate 75 years of service as a district, and meet new people.

The early registration deadline is February 15th, 2023, and the final registration deadline is March 17th, 2023. Registrations received after February 15th will be subject to an additional fee of $25 per registrant. The payment deadline is also March 17th, 2023. The conference fee for Key Club members is $350, and it is $395 for adult advisors and chaperones who will be staying in double occupancy rooms. Single occupancy rooms are also available for $595. Transportation to and from the conference is an additional $10, however, this amount is subject to change, and prepare yourself accordingly. Additionally, if you choose not to use the provided transportation, you will need to make your own arrangements.

There are resources available to help with the cost of attending the conference, and you can also consider organizing fundraisers to offset the cost. If you have any questions or need more information, please don't hesitate to reach out to me.

To register, please use the following link: LINK. You can find a user's guide with step-by-step instructions, as well as all necessary documents and forms, on the conference page of our website: LINK. Clubs should log in using their Club ID as both their username and initial password, and club ID numbers can be found in this document.

We encourage you to promote the conference and encourage Key Club members to join us for this special celebration of 75 years of service. You can use email, social media, a promotional video (once it is made), and the conference page on our website to spread the word. Be sure to emphasize the theme of the Diamond Jubilee, the leadership development opportunities, and the chance to create unforgettable memories.

We look forward to seeing you all in Albany from March 31st to April 2nd for what is sure to be an incredible weekend! If you have any questions, please don't hesitate to contact me.

Key Note Speaker


Samantha Ramirez-Herrera is a dynamic and accomplished individual who has overcome numerous challenges to achieve her dreams. As an undocumented immigrant, she has had to navigate numerous obstacles, but she has never let those challenges hold her back. Instead, she has used them as motivation to succeed and become an entrepreneur, mother, activist, speaker, actress, and storyteller.

She is the founder and CEO of Offtharecord, a creative content agency and digital magazine that aims to inspire others to live, create, and inspire. Through this platform, she shares her own story and encourages others to pursue their own passions and goals.

In addition to her work with Offtharecord, Samantha has also gained recognition for her activism and public speaking. She has appeared on MSNBC's Hardball with Chris Matthews, as well as in various publications such as Forbes, Mundo Hispanico, AFROPUNK, and The Collegian. She has also spoken at a variety of events and institutions, including Emory University's Goizueta Business School, Georgia Hispanic Chamber of Commerce, Atlanta Association of Independent Schools, Los Angeles City College, and HATCH Experience North America.

Samantha's dedication and hard work have been recognized by the Aspen Institute, which named her a 2018 scholar at the Ideas Festival. She is truly an inspiring and motivating individual who has achieved much despite the challenges she has faced.

International Updates

Key Club International has recently launched an initiative called Sustainability Saturday, which is intended to raise awareness about the importance of environmental stewardship. As part of this campaign, an International Trustee will be posting a graphic and a link to an informative article on their Instagram Story every Saturday. These links will be available for viewing on the Linktree, and the graphics will be saved in an Instagram Highlight for easy access at a later time.

In addition to the Sustainability Saturday campaign, Key Club International is currently accepting nominations for the Key of Honor award, which is the highest recognition award presented by the Key Club International board. This award is given to an adult who has made a lifelong positive impact on youth through their involvement in Key Club. If you know of an adult who you believe deserves this recognition, you can submit an application by January 9th, 2023. For more information about the Key of Honor award and how to nominate someone, please click this link.

Key Club International is also offering a variety of awards and contests at various levels, including the member level, club level, division level, district level, and international level. If you are interested in applying for one of these awards or contests, you can find more information and get started by clicking this link.

The second cycle of the Youth Opportunities Fund (YOF) will begin on March 1st, 2023. This fund provides financial support to Key Club members who have a project or idea that they believe will have a positive impact on their communities. If you are a Key Club member and you have a project or idea that you would like to apply for funding through the YOF, keep an eye out for more information as the start date approaches.

GLC

Key Club International (KCI) has released a series of new courses for their Global Leadership Certificate (GLC) program, which is aimed at helping members develop their leadership skills. These new courses provide a fresh perspective for those who have completed previous GLC courses and can be great additions to resumes. The GLC program is designed to help members develop their leadership skills through a series of courses. The courses cover a wide range of topics, including communication, team building, conflict resolution, and project planning. The goal of the GLC program is to provide members with the knowledge and skills they need to become effective leaders within their Key Club and in their communities. Clubs participating in GLC are encouraged to promote it and encourage their members to participate.
